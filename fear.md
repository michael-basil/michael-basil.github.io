[Matzo Ball Soup](https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/3960750/jewish/What-is-a-Matzo-Ball.htm) [Cleansing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cleansing)

*“In many Jewish homes, matzo balls are a [Passover](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Passover) specialty. Since many of our regular sources of carbs are forbidden for the duration of the holiday, matzo balls floating in Bubby’s chicken soup is the stuff of childhood memories.*

***But there is a problem**. Many communities, chassidic ones, in particular, have the custom to refrain from eating gebrokts during the first seven days of Passover. Gebrokts is a Yiddish word that refers to matzah that has come in contact with water. It literally means “**broken**,” and it has come to mean “**wet matzah**” because matzah is usually ground or broken up into crumbs before it is mixed with water.”*

[Matzo Ball Soup](https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/3960750/jewish/What-is-a-Matzo-Ball.htm) is a [Metaphor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metaphor) for life and the [Systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System) around us.  Our friends, work colleagues, and family all have their own *Matzo Ball Soup Systems*.

Sometimes when we are close to people, groups, or organizations our Soup ([System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System)) blends in with other Soup Systems.  This can lead to bad [Tasting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Degustation) and [Smelling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smell) matzo balls in either or both bowls. In some cases, our soups can blend so much that we cannot tell where our soup bowl begins and ends.  Our bowl (system boundaries) has lost [Integrity](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/integrity) (no longer [Autonomous](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/autonomous)).  Because of this the soups become blended ([Enmeshed](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/contemporary-psychoanalysis-in-action/201410/tangled-in-enmeshment)).

Most people would agree that it is [Suboptimal](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/suboptimal) to live with perpetual [Indigestion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indigestion) and [Heartburn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heartburn) if it is not necessary.  People often wonder why [God](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God), in whatever form or [Energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy) concept they [Believe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belief) in, allows for pain and [Suffering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suffering).

Buddhism is more of a philosophy than a religion. It can co-exist with any primary religion practice one may choose to adopt in life.  Or any other set of practices. [Relative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relative) to this [Conversation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socratic_method), the important thing to understand from [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism) is that it is built on the premise to help people find ways to relieve [Suffering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suffering) in life.  One premise is, [Attachment](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/attachment) can lead to [Suffering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suffering).

**Is it [Productive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Productivity) to [Detach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Detachment) from everything in [Life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life)?**

That would be [Myopic](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/myopic) and lead to a [Suboptimal](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/suboptimal) life journey.  Sure, [Emotional Detachment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotional_detachment) from [Material Possessions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Materialism) (cars, houses, fancy apartments, clothes, diamonds, collections) can be very effective to relieve quite a bit of [Suffering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suffering), as [Jealousy](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/jealousy) can ensue when we try to *keep up with* our neighbors.  In my [Experiences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Experience), keeping score with our [Relationships](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relationship) rarely ends well.

[Minimalism](https://www.theminimalists.com), as depicted by the Jedi in [Star Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Wars), is a great start.  However, to have a [Fulfilled](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulfillment) [Human Experience](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_condition) we [Engage](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/engage) with selected [Individuals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomy) and groups in [Intentional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intention), [Productive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Productivity), and [Impactful](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/impactful) ways.  We do this affirmatively by becoming [Empathetic](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/empathy), [Sympathetic](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/sympathy), and [Compassionate](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/compassion) to their life [Soup](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/soup).  To increase the [Strength](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/strength), [Trust](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/trust), and [Genuineness](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/genuine) of the [Relationship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relationship) we [Must](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/must) become [Vulnerable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulnerability) to [Expressing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expression) our [Feelings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feeling) clearly and [Listening](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Listening) to their ideas.  After all [Parties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Party) have been [Heard](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/shift-mind/201009/learning-be-heard), we must [Embrace](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/embrace) a [Willingness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volition_\(psychology\)) to [Disagree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Controversy).  Doing all of this without [Giving](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giving) in to the [Temptation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temptation) to make our [Judgements](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/judgment) through [Blame](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blame), [Pity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pity), and [Shame](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shame).  Only then, will the [Relationship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relationship) remain [foundationally](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/foundation) [Solid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid) and on firm [Footing](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/footing) and [Prepared](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/preparation) for the [Storms](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/storm) that [Pass By](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/This_too_shall_pass).

**More to the [Point](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/point)**

When we find [Ourselves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self) in a [Situation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Situation) where our [Soup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soup) [Bowls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bowls) have been [Compromised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compromise), do not fret, [Embrace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embrace) the [Fear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fear).  With [100%](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/100%25) [Certainty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Certainty), there is [Always](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/always) [Hope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hope). There is an [Algorithm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algorithm) to [Return](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Return) the [Soup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soup) back to [Balance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balance_theory), which many [People](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People) can [Implement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implement) with [Relative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relative) [Ease](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ease).

**[Cautionary](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/cautionary) [Message](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/message)**

You can [Walk The Line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walk_the_Line) towards [Redemption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Shawshank_Redemption), but beware of those who attempt to [Hustle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Hustle) you!

Through this [Process](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/process), many find that the thought of [Discomfort](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/discomfort) we would [Experience](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/experience) consuming these wet matzo balls was not nearly as bad as what we actually experience.  Many times, we find that we actually experience [Joy](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/joy).
