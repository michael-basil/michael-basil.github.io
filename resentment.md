# Divorce as the Eventually Desirable State! 😠😌🤗

What transpires in _Divorce Dynamics_ is unfathomable at times, some say dramatic, some say horrible, some say traumatic, some say ridiculously hilarious, everyone says it is life changing.  It rocks the base of assumptions for not just those going through the experience, but any person, group, or system peripherally involved; including family, friends, co-workers, bosses, subordinates, peers, municipalities, health care workers, and therapists.  Eventually, attorneys enter the situation who were usually referenced to one or both parties to help calm things down.  This is really when things get interesting!

However that sounds, it’s actually quite useful depending on what you do throughout the process and after the judge’s gavel has dropped a few times.  I was lucky, because I was already on a path that would allow me to not feel controlled by the _Divorce Roller Coaster_.  How’s that?  Well, during struggles with conceptualizing anxiety, depression, discomfort, and later divorce I was fortunate to have been introduced to a concept that literally felt like flipping on a light switch.  I still can recall the exact moment in early 2018 when I was shown:

[The Backwards Bicycle: What it takes to Unlearn Old and Learn New Habits](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CqwfGUhYBEA)

I decided to challenge myself using the ideas from the film.  Each time someone presented a reason why I should villainize the other, myself, or the system I stored it up as fuel.   This later propelled me to pursue deep understanding by considering the problem from different perspectives, until I found the _Real Problem_.  I kept repeating the exercise until I could find no more _Real Problem_ at a deeper level of understanding.  I tried utilizing lenses and experts of psychology, neurology, and philosophy.  I even attempted thought exercises from the conflict model from each major religion (at least known or knowable to me).  Later I tried a much simpler approach to consider the reverse framing of humorizing the previous situations.  What does that even mean?  Well, simply this ...

## What would it mean if this entire process and result state was actually fun, not just for me, but for anyone in retrospect?

It was then blaringly clear that **_NOT-PEOPLE_**, but **_Closed Mindsets_** lead to despair, denouncement, disparagement and division:

* Atypical behavior is dangerous
* Money creates happiness
* Divorce is a failed state caused by someone
* Discomfort is to be avoided
* Some people are too far gone to learn or change
* Frustration and anger should be repressed
* Fight to get what is rightfully yours

In relative comparison these **_Open Mindsets_** eventually lead to positivity and **_People-Compassion_**:

* Unexpected occurrences are interesting
* Connections produce powerful networks
* Divorce is like a loud, smelly, expensive fart!
* Discomfort is required for improvement
* If you can laugh, you can learn!
* All emotions are signals with utility
* There is always another eventual renegotiation

**_Resilience through Mindset Recalibration_** takes negative or toxically charged situations and preserves the _Human-Factor_, or as one might say it is _Hard on the Real Problem, Compassionate to the Humans!_  It is an invaluable life skill that anyone can learn.  What is unique about it is that it only takes one person in the group dynamic to commit to this path.  **That one person is YOU**, when you are ready and always of your sole choice in the matter and application of how that is done.  For me as a parent, I aspire that my children Jack and Lila master this skill.  Moreover, it is heart-warming to know that my experiences and this type of approach is not unique.  By engaging the story of the former Google X CEO [Mo Gawdat](https://twitter.com/mgawdat), many come to the same awareness.  If you find yourself complaining that some other person or group is the cause of your unhappiness, please consider the following:
