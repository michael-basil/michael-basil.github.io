## What is Taoism?

The description can only be a pointer, indirect or a reference.  Taoism is not rigid.  Rigorous perhaps, but not static.  There is no Absolute Right.  Context is important.  Trends in the systems around us, inclusive to the dimension of time, create awareness opportunities.  This is where we come to Wu-Wei.  Effortless action and or non action.  I think if this as Good Intuitive Timing.  A person in Flow with their surroundings and the true nature of things.  One who is amped up emotionally or distracted mentally will be the subject of the opposite of Wu-Wei, imposition and or dramatic struggle with other people and forces.

People will manifest behaviors and actions that are effortless in unique ways.  One “Taoist” will not look like another.  Perhaps no one really attuned to what Taoism describes would categorize themselves at all.

What is common to people attenuating their energy to this type of personal discipline must be a cultivated ability to clear their mind.  Letting go of the subjective delusions that are so alluring to us.  This allows a return, Fu perhaps.  The veil of judgement is not exactly real, but it is necessary and productive for one who is not yet mature enough to clearer, smoother, perhaps circular versus angled and sharp thinking.

If Abrahamic and Confusion style religion and or philosophy are dried cement, this would make Taoism be Water in relative comparison.
